What if all relevant data for any property in London was available in an easy to digest format?

Consider the following scenarios...

The Young Professional

One of Londons many upcoming professionals has decided to jump onto the property ladder. They have the money, they know roughly which area they want and have their down payment together

But first our potential customer will have to check prices on other estate agents websites to determine average prices for the area. They will want to see historical data on record keeping websites, as well as future developments in the area, to get an idea for growth once they decide to sell. They may need to calculate their new commute by visiting google maps or other mapping applications, as well as TFL's website.

The Growing Family

A young family move out into the suburbs of London. They find their dream home on your estate agencies website. The garden is big, the road is quiet and there are enough bedrooms for the forseable future.

The family will have too search review websites for information on local schools, and check google for information about parks and greenspace for the children, they will also have to check the local councils website for parking restrictions if they want to get a family car. All these activities will lead to them navigating away from the agencies website.

The Investor

A professional landlord, looking to make another investment is interested in one of your properties. They may be interested in renting the property through your agency, and are experienced in the market.

The professional will need to research a lot of other properties on other sites in the area to gain an understanding of the costs involved with their plan. Are there restrictions on HMO's (houses of multiple occupation) in the area? What is the average price for a room in a HMO in the area? Planning permissions may need to be sought from local authority websites. Finally the entertainment value of the area will have to be investigated through cultural and entertainment websites.

What do all these scenarios have in common?

In every scenario, your potential customer has to leave your website for large amounts of time to find answers to their questions.

And when they leave your website their browser will record their activity and send the data to advertising companies. These companies will pick up on your potential customers interest in London real estate, and from that moment on...

Every ad your potential customer sees in their browser will be for real estate in London.

Ads for your competitors.

And chances are, your potential customer will see something they like, And suddenly your potential customer is no longer your potential customer. But your competitors.

The Solution

London Property Hacker provides all the data any potential customer could want to know, packaged inside interactive widgets. The widgets are easy to implement and install. In fact, each widget can be installed with a single line of HTML code.

Products

Core Widgets

Turbo-charge your inventory page

The core widgets are available as single lines of html code that can be copied onto any webpage. The examples below show data for Stoke Newington

Price Graph Widgets

The price graphs show data for LSOA's (lower super output areas).There are over 600 LSOA's within London which allows for a very fine level of detail. The results can be refined with other variables.

Demand to Buy

This graph shows the number of houses available to purchase vs the number that have been sold in the last month. Results can be refined by number of bedrooms.

Demand to Rent

This graph shows the number of houses available to rent vs the number that have been let in the last month. Results can be refined by number of bedrooms.

Average Selling Price

This graph shows the average price a house sells for in the area. Results can be refined by number of bedrooms or the statistical method (mean or median).

Average Letting Price

This graph shows the average price a house is let for in the area. Results can be refined by number of bedrooms or the statistical method (mean or median).

Time to Rent a Property

This graph shows the average time it takes to rent a property in the area. Results can be refined by number of bedrooms.

Time to Sell a Property

This graph shows the average time it takes to sell a property in the area. Results can be refined by number of bedrooms.

Cost to Rent a Room in a Property in the Area.

This graph shows the cost of a room in the area. Results can be refined by number of bedrooms within the property, statistical method (mean or median) and the size of the room.

Map Based Widgets

The map based widgets provide a visual overview of the surrounding area, allowing users to get a better understanding of the neighbourhood before even visiting.

Local Area Map

The Local Area map shows a snapshot of the closest areas of interest to a property.

Local Area Array Map

The Local Area Array map shows a more detailed view of the local area, with lists of different points of interest.

School Checker

The School Checker provides a detailed overview of near-by schools and allows users to filter results.

Journey Checker

The Journey Checker lets users see the average journey time and cost to any other point within London with a click.

The widgets are easy to implement. Simply copy two lines of HTML onto the desired page and London Property Hacker will automatically do the rest.

REST API

The data that powers the widgets is also available as a REST API. Developers can use the REST API for research or to develop their own apps and widgets. See the following swagger document for details of the calls available.
